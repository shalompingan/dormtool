5 Things You'll Pack and Never Touch (Plus What to Buy Instead)

June 27, 2026Packing Guide7 min read

You've seen the dorm tour videos. The twinkle lights draped like a fairy kingdom. The matching beige comforter set. The "dorm aesthetic" that looks like a West Elm catalog threw up in a 12x12 cinderblock box. Cute content. Real talk? Half that stuff will be shoved under your bed by week two, and you'll be out $300 on things that actively make your life worse.

I've been through it. I've watched freshmen show up with trunk organizers and leave with them still empty at winter break. I've seen the $80 "dorm rug" get destroyed by dining hall floor grease within a month. I've done the walk of shame returning a full garbage bag of useless junk to Target.

Here is your actual what not to bring to college dorm first year checklist — from someone who learned the hard way so you don't have to.

✦ ✦ ✦
Leave at Home

1. A Giant Desk Printer

Why it's a trap: You think you'll print 50 pages a week. You will print six pages all semester. The ink cartridge will dry up, the printer will take up 30% of your desk, and when you finally need to print something, it will be out of cyan or magenta or some other color you didn't know existed. Every dorm building has a free or cheap printing station in the lobby. The library has printers with a built-in quota. Your department office will print things for you if you ask nicely.

Buy Instead

Nothing. You already have printing access.

Take the $80 you would have spent and put it toward a mattress topper. Your back will remember the trade.

Leave at Home

2. Full Set of Dishes and Cookware

Why it's a trap: Your mom buys you a 12-piece dinnerware set from Target. Nice gesture. But you're eating in the dining hall for 90% of your meals. That dining hall swipe already costs you (or your loans) $10–$15 per meal whether you use it or not. You are not going to cook pasta at 11 PM in a communal kitchen that smells like burnt popcorn. You will use exactly one bowl, one mug, one spoon, and one set of chopsticks.

Buy Instead

One large microwave-safe bowl, one spill-proof mug, one set of utensils.

If your dorm allows a mini-fridge, great. If not, you don't need it. The dining hall exists for a reason — use it.

Leave at Home

3. Your Full High School Wardrobe

Why it's a trap: You bring 40 hangers worth of clothes. Your closet is 18 inches wide. You will wear the same 8–10 outfits on eternal rotation because that's what everyone does. Nobody is looking at your fit — they're all stressed about their own classes. The letterman jacket, the formal dress you wore to prom, the three hoodies from clubs you quit — they will sit untouched in a laundry bag until move-out day.

Buy Instead

7 days of casual wear, 2 nicer outfits, 1 rain jacket, a pair of shower flip-flops.

Non-negotiable on the flip-flops — communal shower floors are chemically hazardous. Trust me.

Leave at Home

4. Textbooks Before Syllabus Week

Why it's a trap: You're an overachiever. I was too. I bought "Introduction to Psychology" for $180 before the first class, only for the professor to say "actually we use open-source readings on Canvas this year." Half your professors will change the textbook between when you order it and when the class starts. The other half will tell you the "required" textbook is optional. You will spend $300 on books you crack open zero times.

Buy Instead

Nothing. Wait until Week 2. Rent used.

Go to the first week of classes. Sit down. Listen. On Friday, check the syllabus. If the textbook is truly required, rent it used from Amazon or your campus bookstore. Do not buy new. Do not buy before you know. This one habit will save you more money than any packing decision you make.

Leave at Home

5. A Car (If You're On Campus)

Why it's a trap: Car = parking pass ($300–$800 per year), insurance bump, and the constant anxiety of "did someone hit my bumper in the student lot?" Freshman year, you are on campus for everything — dining hall, classes, library, dorms. You don't need a car. You need a good backpack and maybe a bike. The car will sit in the lot collecting bird droppings while you pay for the privilege.

Buy Instead

A solid backpack with laptop compartment, a 32oz water bottle, and a portable charger.

Put the car money toward a weekend trip with friends instead. You'll get more memories.

TikTok influencers want you to buy the aesthetic trash can they get a commission on.

Nobody is on your side telling you what to skip.
